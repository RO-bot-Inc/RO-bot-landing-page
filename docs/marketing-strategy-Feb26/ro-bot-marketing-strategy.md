# RO-bot Marketing Strategy

## Document Purpose

This document translates the RO-bot positioning (see: RO-bot Website Positioning & Plan) into an actionable marketing strategy. It covers the ad-to-website-to-demo funnel, content marketing, LinkedIn strategy, and near-term execution priorities. Written for Dave and any marketing freelancers or agencies supporting execution.

Last updated: February 2026

---

## Part 1: The Funnel

### How Buyers Find and Evaluate RO-bot

The funnel has three layers, each with a different job:

**Layer 1: Hooks (Ads, content, search, referrals)**
Job: Get the right person to click. These are specific, pain-driven messages that target one problem at a time.

**Layer 2: Website (ro-bot.io)**
Job: Reframe the specific pain as part of a bigger revenue problem, and position RO-bot as the platform that solves it. Earn the demo request.

**Layer 3: Demo and Sales Conversation**
Job: Start with whatever pain brought them in. Expand to the full platform story. Convert to pilot.

This is already how Dave sells in conversations. The marketing strategy simply replicates that motion at scale.

### Hook Messages by Pillar

Each pillar has a set of pain-point hooks that work in ads, blog posts, LinkedIn content, and search.

**Find More Work (MPI)**
- "How many findings are your techs missing on every vehicle?"
- "Voice-first inspections: 10x faster, zero skipped items"
- "Your MPI completion rate is costing you money"
- "What if inspections took 60 seconds instead of 10 minutes?"

**Sell More Work (Video)**
- "Shops lose work when customers don't understand repairs" (tested, top performer)
- "40% of inspection videos never reach the customer. Here's why."
- "Inspection videos your customers will actually watch"
- "How to turn a 'not right now' into an approved estimate"

**Get Paid for More Work (3Cs)**
- "Techs lose nearly an hour a day on paperwork" (tested, top performer)
- "45 minutes typing stories. Zero minutes with RO-bot." (tested, top performer)
- "Poor documentation rejects 12% of warranty claims"
- "What if every tech wrote stories like your best tech?"
- "Your warranty denial rate is a documentation problem"

**Cross-pillar / Platform level**
- "Every RO has more revenue in it"
- "Turn every technician into your most productive, most profitable tech"
- "Stop leaving money on the shop floor"

### Channel Strategy

**Paid ads (Facebook, Google, LinkedIn):** Use pillar-specific hooks. Each ad targets one pain point and drives to the homepage (or a pillar-specific landing page if built). Based on ad test data, prioritize:
1. Revenue/upsell messaging (top performer)
2. Speed/time contrast messaging (strong performer)
3. Warranty recovery messaging (solid performer)

Creative execution matters. AI-assisted ad creative significantly outperformed basic versions of the same messages in testing. Invest in strong visual creative for ads.

**Google Search:** Target high-intent keywords tied to specific pain points. Examples:
- "warranty claim denial rate dealership"
- "auto tech documentation software"
- "repair story writing tool"
- "technician productivity dealership"
- "digital vehicle inspection software"
- "MPI completion rate"

**LinkedIn (organic):** See Part 3 for detailed strategy. Primary channel for thought leadership and direct prospect engagement.

**Blog / SEO:** See Part 2 for content plan. Long-term play to capture organic search traffic from buyers actively searching for solutions.

**Referrals and word of mouth:** Dealership networks (20 Groups, OEM meetings) are a powerful organic channel. The marketing strategy should arm happy customers with shareable proof (case studies, one-pagers) that make it easy to recommend RO-bot.

**Influencer partnerships:** Charles (Humble Mechanic) and other industry creators. Content collaboration that puts RO-bot in front of large, trusted audiences.

## Part 2: Content Marketing Plan

### Content Philosophy

Every piece of content should do one of two things:
1. Pull a buyer in by addressing a specific pain point they're already searching for or thinking about
2. Build credibility and trust that RO-bot (and Dave) understand this industry deeply

Content maps to the three pillars. Each post targets a specific audience (techs, service managers, or fixed ops directors) and a specific stage (awareness, consideration, or decision).

### Priority Content by Pillar

#### Get Paid for More Work (3Cs) -- Highest Priority

This is where you have the most proof and the strongest competitive position. Content here should be rich with data and specifics.

**Blog posts:**
- "What a C+ Repair Story Is Costing Your Dealership" -- Use the report card concept to show how documentation quality directly impacts warranty revenue. Include the grading criteria. Show what an A looks like vs. a C+.
- "We Timed How Long Techs Spend Writing Stories. Here's What We Found." -- Real data from pilots. Concrete time savings. This is the kind of post that gets shared in Fixed Ops Facebook groups.
- "Why Your Best Tech's Stories Still Get Rejected" -- Even experienced techs miss warranty documentation requirements. Explains the problem and naturally leads to the AI grading solution.
- "The Real Cost of 'Good Enough' Documentation" -- Quantify the downstream cost of mediocre stories: warranty denials, rework time for managers, customer-pay conversions lost.

**Case study (when ready):**
- Pilot shop results: time saved, story quality improvement, warranty impact. This becomes the single most important piece of marketing content you produce.

#### Sell More Work (Video) -- Second Priority

The ad data shows this message pulls hard. Content here can be more concept-driven since you don't have customer usage data yet.

**Blog posts:**
- "40% of Inspection Videos Never Reach the Customer. Here's the Real Reason." -- It's not just bad videos. It's service advisors avoiding hard conversations. AI-guided video solves both problems.
- "What Customers Actually Want to See in an Inspection Video" -- Reframe the video quality conversation around customer psychology, not production quality.
- "The Inspection Video That Closes the Sale" -- What makes a video that converts vs. one that gets ignored. Sets up the AI-guided teleprompter as the solution.

#### Find More Work (MPI) -- Third Priority

MPI content should focus on the speed and completeness angle. Voice-first is a strong differentiator to write about even before you have extensive customer data.

**Blog posts:**
- "Your MPI Completion Rate Is Your Biggest Revenue Leak" -- Make the case that incomplete inspections mean missed revenue. Every skipped item is an opportunity that never gets presented to the customer.
- "What Would Your Shop Look Like If Every Inspection Took 60 Seconds?" -- Paint the picture of voice-first MPI. More throughput, more findings, more revenue.

#### Founder / Thought Leadership

These posts build Dave's personal brand and establish credibility.

**Blog posts:**
- "What 500+ Technician Conversations Taught Me About Building Software for the Trades" -- Customer discovery story. Builds trust that RO-bot is built on deep industry understanding.
- "Why I'm Building an AI Company Without Writing a Line of Code" -- The non-engineer founder story. Relevant for the broader startup audience and positions Dave as a thought leader on AI-enabled entrepreneurship.

## Part 3: LinkedIn Strategy

### The Approach

LinkedIn is the primary channel for reaching Fixed Ops Directors and Service Managers directly. The strategy has two tracks:

**Track 1: Content (Dave's personal profile)**
Publish 2-3 posts per week that establish Dave as someone who understands the automotive service industry deeply and is building something real. Content should feel like insights from a founder in the trenches, not a company marketing feed.

**Track 2: Outbound (direct engagement)**
Use content as air cover for direct outreach to prospects via LinkedIn. When a Service Director sees Dave's content in their feed and then gets a personalized connection request, the conversion rate is significantly higher than cold outreach alone.

### Content Mix

**Post Type 1: Industry Insight (1x per week)**
Short, punchy observations about the automotive service industry that demonstrate deep understanding. These are not about RO-bot. They're about the industry.

Examples:
- "I asked a service manager how much warranty denials cost his shop last year. He said 'a few thousand.' The actual number was $187,000."
- "Talked to a tech yesterday who keeps a notebook in his pocket to write down findings because the tablet is too slow. We gave him a $40,000 machine and a $4 notebook is faster."
- "Every Fixed Ops Director I talk to says they want higher MPI completion rates. Almost none of them have measured what their current rate actually is."

**Post Type 2: Building in Public (1x per week)**
Updates on what Dave is learning, building, and shipping. Transparent about challenges. These build trust and attract both customers and the broader founder community.

Examples:
- Share pilot results (with permission): "Our pilot shop saved X hours this week. Here's what we learned."
- Share product decisions: "We built an AI grading system for repair stories. Here's why a report card works better than a style guide."
- Share customer discovery insights: "A warranty admin told me something last week that changed how we think about our product."

**Post Type 3: Blog Amplification (1x per week)**
Take the key insight from the week's blog post and rewrite it as a native LinkedIn post. Don't just link to the blog. Pull out the most compelling data point or story and make it stand alone. Link to the full post at the end for people who want more.

### LinkedIn Content Guidelines

- Write in first person. This is Dave's voice, not a company voice.
- Keep posts under 200 words for industry insights, under 300 for building-in-public updates.
- Open with a line that stops the scroll. Lead with a surprising data point, a contradiction, or a question.
- No hashtag stuffing. One or two relevant hashtags max, or none.
- No emojis.
- No em dashes or en dashes.
- Engage in comments on other people's posts in the automotive service space. This is as important as publishing your own content.

### Outbound Integration

After publishing content consistently for 2-3 weeks, begin targeted outreach:

1. Identify Fixed Ops Directors and Service Managers at target dealerships via LinkedIn Sales Navigator
2. Engage with their content first (comment, like) before sending a connection request
3. Connection request should be personal and specific, not a pitch
4. After connecting, share a relevant piece of content (blog post or insight) that maps to a pain point you suspect they have
5. If they engage, offer a 15-minute demo

The goal is not to pitch cold. The goal is to become a familiar, credible presence in their feed before asking for their time.

## Part 4: Measurement

### What to Track

**Website:**
- Demo requests (primary conversion metric)
- Traffic by source (which channels are driving visits)
- Time on page for key sections (are people reading the pillar content?)
- Bounce rate on homepage (is the new positioning holding attention?)

**Content:**
- Blog post traffic (which topics drive the most visits?)
- Blog-to-demo conversion (which posts lead to demo requests?)
- Search rankings for target keywords (long-term SEO progress)

**LinkedIn:**
- Post impressions and engagement rate
- Profile views from target titles (Fixed Ops Director, Service Manager)
- Connection acceptance rate from outbound efforts
- Demos booked from LinkedIn conversations

**Ads (when running):**
- CTR by message/pillar
- Cost per demo request
- Demo-to-pilot conversion rate

### Monthly Review

At the end of each month, answer these questions:
1. How many qualified demo requests came in, and from where?
2. Which content/message drove the most engagement?
3. Which pillar is generating the most interest?
4. What did we learn from prospect conversations that should change our content or positioning?

## Part 5: Near-Term Priorities (Next 30 Days)

In priority order:

1. **Update the website** based on the Website Positioning & Plan document. This is the foundation everything else builds on. A prospect who arrives at the current site sees a warranty-only story that doesn't reflect the full product or the strongest value prop.

2. **Publish the first blog post** ("What a C+ Repair Story Is Costing Your Dealership"). This leverages your strongest proof point (the report card) and targets the 3Cs buyer directly.

3. **Start LinkedIn content cadence.** Aim for 2 posts in the first week, then build to 3/week. Start with industry insights to build credibility before sharing product-specific content.

4. **Prepare the pilot case study.** Work with your current pilot shop to document results. Even preliminary data is valuable. This becomes the centerpiece of the proof section on the website and the most powerful piece of sales collateral you have.

5. **Set up tracking.** Make sure Google Analytics is capturing demo requests as conversions, and that you can attribute them to source (organic, LinkedIn, ads, referral). You need this data to know what's working.

## Appendix: Messaging Quick Reference

For anyone writing content, ads, or copy for RO-bot, here are the tested and approved messages ranked by proven performance:

### Tier 1: Proven Winners (use liberally)
- "Shops lose work when customers don't understand repairs" (revenue/video angle)
- "Techs lose nearly an hour a day on paperwork" (time/productivity angle)
- "45 minutes typing stories. Zero minutes with RO-bot." (speed contrast)
- "Turn every technician into your most productive, most profitable tech" (platform promise)
- "Every RO has more revenue in it" (revenue umbrella)

### Tier 2: Strong Supporting Messages
- "Find more work. Sell more work. Get paid for more work." (value framework)
- "Better stories. Zero typing." (3Cs shorthand)
- "12% of warranty claims get denied. The #1 cause is bad documentation."
- "Faster techs. Better documentation. More revenue per RO."

### Tier 3: Use Selectively
- Warranty-specific messaging (strong for the right audience, but not the lead)
- AI/technology messaging (use as "how," not "what" or "why")
- Technician adoption / satisfaction messaging (secondary benefit, not primary)

### Words and Phrases to Avoid
- "Revolutionize" or "revolutionary"
- "Game-changer"
- "Cutting-edge" or "state-of-the-art"
- "Seamless" or "seamlessly"
- "Leverage" (as a verb)
- "Harness the power of"
- "Empower" or "empowering"
- "Robust"
- "Solution" (overused; be specific about what it actually does)
- Em dashes and en dashes
- Emojis

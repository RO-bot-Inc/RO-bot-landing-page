# RO-bot Website Positioning & Plan

## Document Purpose

This document captures the strategic positioning decisions and website plan for ro-bot.io, developed through a structured workshop using April Dunford's positioning framework. It is designed to guide website development and can be shared with freelancers or agencies executing the build.

Last updated: February 2026

---

## Part 1: Strategic Positioning

### Positioning Framework (Dunford Method)

#### 1. Competitive Alternatives

What would our customers do if RO-bot didn't exist?

**For 3Cs / Story Writing:**
- Status quo: technicians type their own stories manually (slow, error-prone, hated by techs)
- PencilWrench: desktop-only, point-and-click documentation with pre-written phrases. No voice, no AI, no mobile.
- General AI tools: some techs experimenting with ChatGPT. No automotive context, no grading, no workflow.

**For MPI / Video Inspections:**
- Established service lane platforms already deployed in most dealerships: Xtime, MyKaarma, Text2Drive, DealerFX, iService, TruVideo, SingleThread, Autopoint, Kimoby
- These tools already handle digital inspections and video. RO-bot must demonstrate significant benefits over what they do today.

**Key insight:** On 3Cs, RO-bot is creating a new category. On Video/MPI, RO-bot is displacing incumbents. The 3Cs positioning is easier to prove and defend today. The Video/MPI story is stronger emotionally but requires more proof.

#### 2. Differentiated Capabilities

What does RO-bot have that the alternatives do not?

- **Voice-first input across all workflows.** Techs talk instead of type or tap. No other tool in this market is voice-first.
- **AI-generated 3Cs stories.** Instant, professional, warranty-compliant documentation from voice notes. Not pre-written phrases (PencilWrench), not generic AI (ChatGPT).
- **Automatic story grading.** 7-criteria report card that scores every story within 5 seconds. Gives techs a feedback loop and gives managers visibility into documentation quality across the shop.
- **AI-guided video inspections.** Shot list planning and on-screen teleprompter so techs know what to show and what to say. No other inspection tool does this.
- **Voice-first MPI.** Techs call out findings as they go. No clipboard, no buttons, no tapping through checklists.
- **Zero integration required.** Up and running in 10 minutes. No DMS integration needed. Techs copy/paste output into whatever system they already use.
- **10-minute onboarding.** Minimal training burden for managers trying to roll out a new tool.

#### 3. Differentiated Value

So what? What do these capabilities mean for the buyer's business?

- **Techs save 30+ minutes per day** on story writing. That time goes back into billable work.
- **Better documentation quality** means fewer warranty denials, fewer claim rejections, less rework for managers chasing techs to fix stories.
- **Higher MPI completion rates** mean more findings discovered per vehicle, which means more recommended work presented to customers.
- **Better inspection videos** mean higher customer approval rates on recommended work. Shops lose 30-40% of recommended work when customers decline. Video that educates and builds trust closes that gap.
- **Managers get visibility** into documentation quality without micromanaging, through the grading system.
- **Better 3Cs also improve customer-pay conversions.** More comprehensive, professional repair stories help service advisors explain value to customers, not just warranty administrators.

**Revenue math for the buyer:**
- Reduced warranty denials: recovering portion of $200K average annual loss
- More billable hours per tech per day (30 min reclaimed = ~$40K per tech per year in productive time)
- Higher approval rates on customer-pay work (20%+ increase in customer-pay revenue is the target)

#### 4. Best-Fit Customer Segment

Who cares the most about this value?

**Primary buyer:** Fixed Ops Director at a small-to-medium dealer group (10-20 stores)

**Characteristics that make them care more:**
- High warranty volume with above-average denial rates (15%+)
- Recently went through or is concerned about a warranty audit
- Service advisors struggling with customer-pay approval rates
- Technician retention challenges (documentation burden contributing to turnover)
- Forward-thinking about technology; willing to try new solutions
- Has decision-making authority for purchases under $1K/month per store without lengthy corporate approval

**Secondary buyer:** Service Manager at a small-to-medium dealer group
- More operational focus; cares about tech productivity and daily workflow
- Often the person who discovers RO-bot and champions it up to the Fixed Ops Director

**The user (not the buyer):** Automotive technician
- Ideal early adopter: under 35, tech-comfortable
- Motivated by: earning more money, spending less time on screens
- Key adoption insight: techs won't adopt another tool unless it clearly makes them more money or saves them significant time. "Show me how this puts more money in my pocket."

#### 5. Market Category

What is RO-bot?

**Current positioning:** "AI Co-Pilot for Auto Technicians" -- this is vague and doesn't trigger strong assumptions about value.

**Recommended positioning:** Voice-first AI platform for automotive service departments.

This category frames RO-bot as:
- A platform (not a single-feature tool), which supports the multi-pillar value story
- Voice-first (the key differentiator across all features)
- AI-powered (leveraging the trend without leading with it)
- For service departments (the buyer's world, not just the tech's world)

**Trend layer:** AI in automotive service is a legitimate trend that helps buyers understand urgency. But we use it as context, not as the headline. The headline is about revenue and productivity. AI is how we deliver it.

### The One-Sentence Positioning

**Turn every technician into your most productive, most profitable tech.**

This works because:
- It speaks to the buyer's core desire (consistent high performance across the team)
- It's about the outcome, not the technology
- It resonates with both Fixed Ops Directors ("most profitable") and Service Managers ("most productive")
- It doesn't box RO-bot into a single feature or workflow

### The Value Framework

**Umbrella:** Voice-first AI tools that help your techs find more work, sell more work, and get paid for more work.

| Pillar | Workflow | Value to Buyer | Value to Tech |
|--------|----------|---------------|---------------|
| Find More Work | Voice-first MPI | Higher completion rates = more opportunities discovered | Faster inspections, no clipboard |
| Sell More Work | AI-guided Video Inspections | Higher approval rates on customer-pay work | Guided process, no guessing what to say |
| Get Paid for More Work | AI-powered 3Cs | Fewer warranty denials, better customer-pay stories | Zero typing, instant stories, more wrench time |

## Part 2: Website Plan

### Strategic Approach

The website serves buyers who arrive through two paths:

**Path 1: Specific pain.** They clicked an ad or blog post about a specific problem (warranty denials, story writing time, inspection completion rates). They land on the site looking for a solution to that problem.

**Path 2: General discovery.** They heard about RO-bot from a colleague, at a 20 Group meeting, or through an influencer. They want to understand what it is.

Both paths need to work. The site should lead with the broad platform story (which elevates Path 1 visitors and orients Path 2 visitors), then provide enough specificity in each pillar section to satisfy buyers with a particular pain point.

### Evidence Strategy

RO-bot has strong proof for 3Cs (active pilots, time savings data, report card feature, user feedback). Video and MPI features are live but do not yet have customer usage data. The website should reflect this honestly:

- **3Cs section:** Heavy on proof. Specific metrics, report card screenshot, user quotes, time savings data.
- **Video section:** Lead with the concept and the problem it solves. Describe the AI-guided capability. Use proof from the ad test insight ("shops lose work when customers don't understand repairs"). As customer data comes in, add it.
- **MPI section:** Lead with the speed story and the voice-first differentiator. Describe the workflow. Add proof as pilots generate data.

This approach is honest without being apologetic. Every capability described is real and live. The proof weighting just reflects where you are.

### Page Structure: Homepage

#### Section 1: Hero

**Headline:** Turn Every Technician Into Your Most Productive, Most Profitable Tech

**Subhead:** RO-bot is voice-first AI that helps your techs find more work, sell more work, and get paid for more work.

**CTA:** Book Your Demo

**Visual direction:** Show the RO-bot app on a phone in a shop environment. The visual should communicate "mobile," "shop floor," and "voice" without needing to explain it.

#### Section 2: The Problem

**Section headline:** Your Service Department Is Leaving Money on the Table

This section should paint the picture of revenue leaking from the service workflow at every stage. Not a wall of stats, but a narrative that a Fixed Ops Director recognizes as their daily reality.

**Key points to weave in (as narrative, not bullet points):**

Rushed inspections mean missed findings. Findings that never get discovered are revenue that never gets recommended.

Inspection videos that aren't clear or compelling don't get sent to the customer. Or the customer watches but doesn't understand what they're looking at. Declined work is revenue that walked out the door.

Techs spend 45+ minutes a day typing stories. That's billable time lost. And the stories they rush through get kicked back by warranty, costing the manager and the tech even more time.

12% of warranty claims get denied. The number one cause is incomplete or poorly written documentation.

**Visual direction:** Consider a simple visual showing revenue "leaking" at each stage of the service workflow: inspection, customer communication, documentation/claims.

#### Section 3: The Solution Framework

**Section headline:** Faster Techs. Better Documentation. More Revenue Per RO.

Brief introduction: RO-bot gives your technicians voice-first AI tools across the entire service workflow. They talk instead of type. AI handles the rest.

Then break into three sub-sections:

**Pillar 1: Find More Work**
Voice-First Multi-Point Inspections

Techs call out findings as they go. No clipboard, no buttons, no skipped items. Voice-first MPI means higher completion rates, which means more opportunities discovered on every vehicle that rolls through your shop.

*Visual: Show the MPI workflow on phone. Emphasize simplicity and speed.*

**Pillar 2: Sell More Work**
AI-Guided Video Inspections

Inspection videos that customers actually watch and approve. RO-bot plans the shot list and guides the tech with an on-screen teleprompter, so every video is clear, professional, and focused on what the customer needs to understand.

*Visual: Show the teleprompter/guided video interface. If possible, show a before/after of a typical inspection video vs. an AI-guided one.*

**Pillar 3: Get Paid for More Work**
AI-Powered 3Cs Documentation

Techs talk. RO-bot writes. Voice notes become professional, warranty-compliant repair stories in seconds. Every story gets graded on 7 criteria so techs know exactly how to improve, and managers get visibility into documentation quality across the shop.

*Visual: Show the report card UI. This is a "magic moment" -- the grading feature is visually compelling and immediately communicable. Consider an animation or before/after showing a voice note transforming into a graded story.*

#### Section 4: Proof

**Section headline:** Results from Real Shops

This section should lead with 3Cs proof (strongest data) and grow over time.

**Content to include now:**
- Time savings: "Techs using RO-bot save 30+ minutes per day on story writing"
- Quality improvement: show the report card and explain how grading drives better documentation
- User/manager quotes from pilot shops
- The speed contrast: "45 minutes typing stories vs. seconds with RO-bot"

**Content to add as it becomes available:**
- MPI completion rate improvements
- Video inspection approval rate data
- Warranty denial rate reductions with specific numbers

**Visual direction:** The report card screenshot is your strongest visual proof asset. Feature it prominently. Consider showing a real (anonymized) before/after: a typical tech-written story vs. a RO-bot generated story.

#### Section 5: How It Works

**Section headline:** Up and Running in 10 Minutes

Keep this simple. Three steps:

1. Download the app. No DMS integration required.
2. Techs record voice notes while they work.
3. RO-bot handles the rest: inspection reports, video guidance, graded 3Cs stories.

**Key message:** This isn't a six-month IT project. Your techs can start using RO-bot today.

*Visual: Show the simplicity. App download, quick onboarding, first voice note, first generated story.*

#### Section 6: Close / CTA

**Headline:** Every RO Has More Revenue In It. Let Us Show You How Much.

**Subhead:** Book a 15-minute demo and see how RO-bot works with your team's real workflow.

**CTA:** Book Your Demo

**Form fields:** Name, Email, Shop/Dealership Name (keep it simple, same as current)

### Supporting Pages

#### About Page
Update to reflect the broader platform positioning. Keep the "What We Believe" structure but ensure it maps to the new value framework. Remove references to features no longer in the product ("Specs At Your Fingertips," "Smarter Diagnostics").

#### Blog (Wrench Time Reports)
The blog should support the website positioning through content that targets specific buyer pain points. Each post should map to one of the three pillars. See the Marketing Strategy document for the content plan.

The blog page itself needs more content. Current state feels thin with only a few posts. Priority is publishing new content aligned to the updated positioning.

#### Feature / Product Pages (Optional, Future)
As each pillar matures with customer proof, consider dedicated pages for MPI, Video, and 3Cs that go deeper than the homepage sections. These become landing pages for targeted ad campaigns.

## Part 3: Copy and Visual Guidelines

### Voice and Tone

- Write for a Fixed Ops Director who has 30 seconds to decide if this is worth their time
- Lead with outcomes, not features
- Use the language of the shop floor: ROs, wrench time, bays, stories, warranty claims
- Be direct and concrete. Avoid vague claims.
- Avoid em dashes and en dashes
- Avoid emojis
- Avoid AI-generated writing patterns: no "game-changer," "revolutionize," "cutting-edge," "seamless," "leverage" (as a verb), "harness the power of"
- Use "AI" sparingly. It's a capability, not the headline.
- OK to be confident. This audience respects directness.

### Headline Hierarchy

| Level | Purpose | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| H1 (Hero) | The big promise | Turn Every Technician Into Your Most Productive, Most Profitable Tech |
| H1 Support | How we deliver | Voice-first AI that helps your techs find more work, sell more work, and get paid for more work |
| H2 (Sections) | Frame each section | Your Service Department Is Leaving Money on the Table |
| H3 (Pillars) | Name each capability | Find More Work / Sell More Work / Get Paid for More Work |
| CTA | Drive action | Every RO Has More Revenue In It. Let Us Show You How Much. |

### Key Visual Assets Needed

1. **Hero image/video:** RO-bot app in use in a real shop environment. Should communicate mobile + voice + shop floor.
2. **Report card UI:** The 7-criteria grading screen. This is the single most compelling visual asset you have right now. Feature it prominently in the 3Cs section and potentially in the proof section.
3. **3Cs before/after:** A side-by-side showing a typical hand-typed tech story vs. a RO-bot generated story.
4. **Video inspection UI:** The teleprompter / guided video interface.
5. **MPI workflow:** The voice-first inspection flow on phone.
6. **Revenue leakage visual:** A simple graphic showing where money is lost across the service workflow (optional but could be powerful in the problem section).
7. **Speed contrast graphic:** The "45 minutes vs. seconds" comparison that tested well in ads. Adapt for the website.

### What to Remove from Current Site

- "Specs At Your Fingertips" section (feature no longer in product)
- "Smarter Diagnostics, Less Guesswork" section (feature no longer in product)
- Exclusive focus on warranty as the primary value prop (warranty becomes one of three pillars)
- Any copy that positions RO-bot as only a documentation tool

### What to Keep from Current Site

- "Book Your Demo" CTA structure and form
- Brand logos (Honda, Ford, Chevy, etc.) showing cross-manufacturer support
- The overall clean, professional design aesthetic
- Footer structure and legal pages

## Part 4: Validation Checklist

Before launching the new site, pressure-test against these questions:

- [ ] Does a Fixed Ops Director understand what RO-bot does within 5 seconds of landing on the page?
- [ ] Does the site clearly answer "why RO-bot instead of what we're doing today?"
- [ ] Is the 3Cs section loaded with specific, credible proof?
- [ ] Are the Video and MPI sections compelling without overclaiming?
- [ ] Does the site work for someone who came via a 3Cs-specific ad? A video-specific ad? A general referral?
- [ ] Is the CTA clear and low-friction?
- [ ] Would a technician who sees this site understand "what's in it for me?"
- [ ] Does anything on the site promise something the product can't deliver today?

## Appendix: Ad Test Insights

A 14-execution Facebook ad test (via Orchard) across two audiences (Car Dealership Workers and General Population) revealed the following performance hierarchy:

**Top performers (significantly above 100 baseline):**
- AI Upsell Revenue: 167 (video/revenue message: "Shops lose work when customers don't understand repairs")
- AI Speed Comparison: 150 (time contrast: "45 minutes / 8 minutes")
- AI Time Drain: 140 (productivity message: "Techs lose nearly an hour a day on paperwork")
- AI Warranty Revenue Loss: 130 (warranty recovery message)

**At or below baseline:**
- All non-AI-created ad versions of similar themes (71-89)
- Video-specific ads focused on process rather than revenue (66-84)
- Customer Trust messaging (66)

**Key takeaways applied to website:**
1. Revenue framing outperforms documentation/quality framing
2. Concrete speed comparisons (45 min vs. 5 min) are highly engaging
3. Video resonates when framed as a revenue tool, not a process improvement
4. Creative execution matters as much as the message itself
5. Dealership workers are harder to engage (0.46% CTR vs 1.55% general) but respond to the same top messages
